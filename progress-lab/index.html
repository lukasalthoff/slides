Stanford Progress Lab
01

How have writing technologies shaped societal progress?

Unlocking hundreds of millions of archaeological artifacts to understand how information technology has shaped human progress over 10,000 years
  • AI extracts material, function, and provenance from artifact records at scale
  • Build the first global, machine-readable map of writing systems and their diffusion
  • Quantify when, where, and why information technologies spread, and what they enabled
  • Link writing technologies to the size and complexity of states and economies around the world over thousands of years
02

How has the internet shaped global scientific collaboration?

Leveraging the internet's global spread to link universities and trace the takeoff of collaboration for discovery
  • Geocoded internet rollouts linked to university addresses across 50+ years
  • Authors, institutions, and papers linked across hundreds of millions of works
  • Causal identification of how connectivity reshapes co-authorship and discovery
03

How is AI changing scientific discovery?

Mapping hundreds of millions of works across every field and era to measure how AI is reshaping discovery itself
  • 5 domains → 30 fields → 300 subfields → 4,500+ topics → 7,800+ tasks
  • Embeddings-based field classifications, author disambiguation, and task taxonomies on top of OpenAlex
  • Case studies of specific AI tools like AlphaFold, as well as general-purpose technologies like Claude Code
  • Enables the first structural studies of how AI is changing research itself
04

How will AI reshape labor market inequality?

Predicting AI's long-run impact on wages and inequality through a dynamic task-based model of skills, occupational choice, and general equilibrium
  • Adds simplification to the channels through which technology affects workers, alongside automation and augmentation
  • Multidimensional skills determine comparative advantage across tasks and shape dynamic occupational choice
  • Estimation recovers skills → task productivity, the law of motion for skill accumulation, and occupational choice
  • Predicts substantial long-run wage gains and a sharp drop in inequality, driven almost entirely by task simplification
05

How is AI reshaping healthcare occupations?

Identifying hospital AI adoption from Medicare claims and tracing impacts on healthcare workers across clinical hierarchies through event studies
  • Medicare claims that flag AI usage pinpoint hospital-level adoption events
  • Event-study estimates of impacts on nurses, technicians, physicians, and administrators over time
  • Tests where AI substitutes, complements, or restructures work along the clinical hierarchy
Data in Progress

Open data projects

1

Scaling automated record linkage

  • Building representative linked census records that include women, not just men
  • AI-enabled transcription of historical censuses (1850–1950), fixing errors and opening access
  • Privacy-preserving linkage of modern US Census records inside the US Census Bureau (1950–2010)
  • Multi-source linkages spanning Census, Veteran Administration, military administrative, and Wikipedia
2

Unleashing multi-modal unstructured data

  • AI-structured Wikidata and museum catalogs: translation, image-to-data, and standardization at scale
  • Uncovering cultural attributes from political speeches, newspapers, and other texts
3

Uncovering the anatomy of science

  • Hundreds of millions of works from OpenAlex, cleaned and refined for structural analysis
  • SciNet task taxonomy derived from scientific protocols, papers, and patents
  • Researcher surveys on tool and AI use, time allocation, and how it's changing
